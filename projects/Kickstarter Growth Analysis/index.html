Kickstarter Growth Analysis

Comprehensive analysis of Kickstarter crowdfunding campaigns from 2009-2012, identifying key success factors and growth trends.

Project Overview

This in-depth analysis examines the growth and performance of Kickstarter crowdfunding campaigns from 2009 to 2012, a formative period for the platform. The study investigates various factors that influence campaign success rates, funding amounts, and backer engagement.

By analyzing historical Kickstarter data, this project identifies trends in category performance, optimal funding goals, campaign duration impacts, and geographical distribution of successful projects. The insights provide valuable guidance for creators looking to launch effective crowdfunding campaigns.

Project Type Data Analytics
Tools Used Excel, PowerPoint, Data Visualization
Date 2022

Key Findings

Growth Patterns

Identified exponential growth in Kickstarter campaigns between 2009-2012, with a 500% increase in total funding volume and significant upward trends in average pledge amounts.

Category Analysis

Discovered that Games, Technology, and Design categories had the highest success rates and funding amounts, while Dance and Theater showed the highest success percentages despite lower funding goals.

Success Factors

Analyzed the correlation between funding goals, campaign duration, and success rates, finding that campaigns with moderate goals (under $10,000) and durations of 30 days or less had significantly higher success rates.
